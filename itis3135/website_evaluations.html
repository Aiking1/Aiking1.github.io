Website Evaluations

The following website Evaluations are besided of the following CRAP principles when designing a webpage.

Delta Airlines

Delta Airlines' website is a great example of clean, modern design. It’s easy to navigate, visually appealing, and makes booking flights or checking in a smooth process.

  • C - ontrast: The colors, fonts, and layout create a clear and professional look. Important elements stand out, making it easy to find what you need.
  • R - epetition: The site maintains a consistent style throughout, from navigation menus to buttons and typography.
  • A - lignment: Everything is well-organized in a structured grid, ensuring a balanced and readable design.
  • P - roximity: Related information, like flight booking options and travel details, are grouped logically, making the site intuitive to use.

Overall, Delta’s site does a fantastic job following CRAP principles, creating a smooth and professional user experience.

Ling's Cars

Ling’s Cars is... an experience. The website is loud, cluttered, and full of flashing graphics, making it overwhelming to navigate.

  • C - ontrast: There’s a lot of it—but not in a good way. Bright, clashing colors and excessive animations make it hard to focus.
  • R - epetition: There’s little consistency. Fonts, colors, and layout styles change randomly across the pages.
  • A - lignment: The content is all over the place, with no real structure or grid to guide the eye.
  • P - roximity: Everything feels crammed together with little spacing, making it difficult to process information.

Ling’s Cars is a perfect example of what not to do when designing a website. It’s fun in a chaotic way, but it completely ignores CRAP principles, making for a frustrating user experience.
